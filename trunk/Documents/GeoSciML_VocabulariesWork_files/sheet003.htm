Average roundness of some collection of particles. Roundness is a measure of the sharpness of the edges between surfaces bounding a particle (see Jackson, 1997; Wadell, 1932). Geologists use different vocabularies for igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks. Roundness is quantitatively defined as the average ratio of the radius of curvature of grain corners (intersection of relatively straight edges) to the radius of the smallest circle that will circumscribe the grain for all corners in the silhouette of the maximum projection plane of the particle.
