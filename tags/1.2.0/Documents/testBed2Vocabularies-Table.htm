GeoSciML vocabulary requirements

Table 1 enumerates 42 vocabularies that are required by the CGI GeoSciML schema for instance documents.  Each vocabulary has a brief statement of its purpose with some example terms. Table 2 provides information on how the vocabularies are used in the GeoSciML v 1.1 schema.

 

Document history:

Compiled by SMR, based on Ottawa working group meeting 09/28/05; Revised, updated 05/12/06 for GeoSciML 1.1

Table 1. Vocabularies required by GeoSciML 1.1 test bed implementation.

Vocabulary Name

Notes

Quantity value qualifier

Term that qualifies the specified value. Examples include greaterThan ( ), lessThan (<), equalTo (=), approximate;  commonly used with numeric values. With term values, use:  never, rare, sometimes, common, always. Also includes nil values: nil:inapplicable, nil:missing, nil:unknown, nil:withheld

Value type semantics

Union elements have attribute that specifies the kind of element implemented by any particular instance of the union element. In this case, populated with string, allowed values are :"value" and "range". This is a flag to soft type a term or numeric property value as either a single value or a range value.

Property types

vocabulary of kinds of physical properties

Relative measure type

enumeration.  Values: "lessThan", "lessThanOrEquals", "equals", "greaterThanOrEquals", "greaterThan”, "nil:inapplicable", "nil:missing", "nil:unknown", "nil:withheld". Default is "equals"

Composition part role

Defines the relationship of the earth material constituent in the geologic unit, e.g. vein, interbedded constituent, layers, dominant constituent. Scoped name because role is asserted by the geologist building the description

Composition part proportion terms

CGI_ValuePropertyType allows a CGI_TermValue, which may be an uncontrolled string or a scoped name. This vocabulary provides terms that may be used to qualitatively express abundance of composition parts in a geologic unit or earth material description. Quantity that specifies the fraction of the geologic unit composed of the compound material. Might use vocabulary terms like abundant, common, sparse, rare.

Consolidation degree terms

A property that specifies the degree to which an aggregation of particles is a distinct solid material. Consolidation and induration are related concepts specified by this property. They define a continuum from non-cemented collections of loose particles to very hard rock. Induration is the degree to which a consolidated material is made hard, operationally determined by how difficult it is to break a piece of the material. Consolidated materials may have varying degrees of induration (NADMSC, 2004)

Metamorphic grade terms

The intensity or rank of metamorphism.   Indicates in a general way the P-T environment in which the metamorphism took place.  May be applied to an EarthMaterial or GeologicUnit.  Determination of metamorphic grade is based on mineral assemblages present in a rock that are interpreted to have crystallized in equilibrium during a particular metamorphic event (NADMSC, 2004)

Particle geometry terms

Quantifies a bulk characterization of particles in an aggregate, independent of their relationship to each other or orientation.  ParticleGeometry describes the particle size, particle size distribution, and particle shape, and particle shape distribution.  ParticleGeometry may have properties such as: particle sorting, size, size variation, shape, and possibly others.  It is applied to megascopic and microscopic features best seen on the smooth surface of a rock or within an unconsolidated material. ParticleGeometry is distinguished from fabric in that the ParticleGeometry description of a CompoundMaterial remains constant if the material is disaggregated into its constituent particles, whereas fabric is lost if the material is disaggregated (NADMSC, 2004). Requires vocabularies for particle size, particle sorting, particle sphericity, particle roundness in granular rocks, and vocabulary for degree of crystal development (e.g. euhedral, subhedral, anhedral) in crystalline rocks.

Fabric terms

Penetrative GeologicStructure that characterizes the compound material. Fabric is defined by the relationship between constituent elements in an EarthMaterial.   Fabric denotes a pattern, defined by one or fabric elements, that is present throughout a rock body when considered at some scale. Fabric is defined based on the average configuration of many constituents. Penetrative denotes that these constituents are distributed throughout the rock volume at the scale of observation [Passchier and Trouw, 1998], and are repeated at distances that are small relative to the scale of thbe whole, such that they can be considered to pervade the whole uniformly and be present at every point (Turner and Weiss [1963] p. 21-24; Hobbs and others [1976], p. 73; Jackson [1997]; Passchier and Trouw [1998]). Fabric is distinguished from ParticleGeometry based on the criteria that ParticleGeometry is preserved if a CompoundMaterial is disaggregated, while Fabric is not defined if the material is disaggregated (NADMSC, 2004)

Compound material gross chemistry terms

Term to specify the gross chemical character of a compound material. Examples include silicate, carbonate, ferromagnesian, oxide... May have different terminology for different kinds of rocks; for example sandstone petrographic classification terms (e.g. feldspathic quartz arenite) might be placed here.

Compound material genesis terms

Term that represents a summary geologic history of the material. Content model for term definitions from a controlled vocabulary of these terms would be like an NADM C1 genesis-- an ordered aggregation of GeologicEvent objects, each of which may have an associated GeologicAge, GeologicEnvironment, and one or more GeologicProcess objects.  Examples include igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic, shock metamorphic, volcanic, pyroclastic...

 

Geologic contact type terms

Terms to classify the type of contact (e.g. intrusive, unconformity, bedding surface, compositional layering, phase boundary) between geologic units.

 

Contact character terms

The character of the boundary between geologic units, as opposed to its type e.g. abrupt, gradational

 

Earth Material color terms

Terms to specify color of material.

 

Earth material physical property type terms

Need vocabulary of physical properties that might be specified for an EarthMaterial, e.g. density, magnetic susceptibility. This vocabulary populates the ‘property’ element of the CGI_PhysicalDescription that specifies the physicalProperty element.

 

Fault movement type terms

Terms to specify the type of fault movement  (e.g. dip-slip, strike-slip, oblique slip).Used for Fault and Fault System.

 

Fault movement sense terms

Terms to specify the sense of movement on a fault (e.g. dextral, sinistral, reverse)

 

Geologic event type terms

This vocabulary will include typical events that may be associated with ages for geologic units or structures, e.g. crystallization, intrusion, cooling through U-Pb zircon closure temperature, cooling through biotite K-Ar closure temperature, cooling through fission track partial annealing zone, deposition, diagenetic recrystallization, peak metamorphic conditions attained….

Named time ordinal era terms

A geologic age value may be specified using a term that is the name of an era in a geologic time scale, e.g. Jurassic, Cretaceous…

Required vocabulary is a standard geologic time scale.

Thematic accuracy terms

Confidence that specified value is accurate. Examples: high, standard, low. See ISO 19115 DQ_ThematicAccuracy

Feature observation method terms

Term(s) that specify the method by which the value was obtained (e.g. point count, brunton compass on site, air photo interpretation, field estimate...)

Description purpose terms

Specification of the intended purpose/level of abstraction for a given feature or object instance. Scoped name because intension is asserted by author of the data instance. Values: Instance, TypicalNorm, IdentifyingNorm.

Geologic relationship role type terms

Specification of semantics of relationship between GeologicEntities associated by a relationship instance.

Geologic structure genesis terms

Geologic structure genesis property may be specified with a term that identifies some particular genetic history that characterizes the structure. This term may come from a NADM controlled vocabulary in which the terms are associated with prototype GeologicEntities of type ‘Genesis’, or may simply be terms with text definitions. Examples include sedimentary, primary sedimentary, penecontemporaneous sedimentary, biogenic sedimentary, primary igneous, tectonic, solution-collapse… Classification of geologic structures according to some sequence of named events or tectonic environments would be through these terms, e.g. ‘Laramide back arc contraction’

Geologic unit part role terms

Relationship between the part and the whole geologic unit, e.g. stratigraphic member, border phase

Geologic unit part proportion terms

Quantity that specifies the fraction of the geologic unit formed by the part. Term examples: dominant, subordinate, minor, rare.. CGI_ValueProperty allows a numeric specification of proportion or a qualitative proportion term; for analysis the prototype description associated with these terms would have to specify some default numeric value to use.

Geologic unit body morphology terms

The geometry or form of a GeologicUnit.  Examples include: dike, cone, fan, sheet, etc. Morphology is independent of the substance (EarthMaterial) that composes the GeologicUnit.

Geologic unit outcrop character terms

Describes the nature of outcrops formed by a geologic unit.   Examples: bouldery, cliff-forming, ledge-forming, slope-forming, poorly exposed

Geologic unit genesis terms

Term that identifies some particular genetic history that characterizes the geologic unit. This term may come from a NADM controlled vocabulary in which the terms are associated with prototype GeologicEntities of type ‘Genesis’, or may simply be terms with text definitions. Examples include Sedimentary, Fluvial, Aeolian, Metamorphic, igneous volcanic, pyroclastic, igneous plutonic…

Geologic unit exposure color terms

Terms that may be used to specify the color of typical outcrops of a geologic unit.

Geologic unit gross chemistry terms

Term to specify the gross chemical character of geologic unit. Examples include silicate, carbonate, ferromagnesian, oxide.  Chemical classification terms for igneous rocks also go here, e.g. alkalic, subaluminous, peraluminous, mafic, felsic, intermediate.

Lithologic unit stratigraphic rank term

term classifies unit in generalization hierarchy from most local/smallest volume to most regional. Scoped name because classification is asserted, not based on observational data.

Lithologic unit outcrop weathering character terms

A description of the nature of the GeologicUnit at its interface with the atmosphere.  Soil associated with a particular GeologicUnit would be described here, as would color and secondary mineral formation on a weathered surface. Examples: saprolitic, resistant, friable, pitted, weak desert varnish

Lithologic unit internal structure type terms

Terms specifying the kinds of small-scale structures that are characteristic of the geologic unit, e.g. herringbone crossbedding, mudcracks, graded bedding, planar lamination, miarolitic cavities, nebulitic structure

Stratified unit bedding style terms

Term(s) specifying the style of bedding in a stratified geologic unit, e.g. lenticular, irregular, planar, vague, massive

Stratified unit bedding pattern terms

Term(s) specifying patterns of bedding thickness or relationships between bedding packages, e.g. thinning upward, thickening upward

Stratified unit bedding thickness terms

Term(s) or numeric values characterizing the thickness of bedding in the unit. Examples thin bedded, medium bedded, thick bedded.

Mapped feature position accuracy terms

Term or number that specifies the accuracy of the spatial coordinates that locate a mapped feature. Example terms: approximate, meets map accuracy standard. CGI value property allows numeric or qualitative term specification; the terms should have associated prototype descriptions that specify default numeric position uncertainty to use for analysis.

Mapped feature observation method terms

Term specifying the method used to locate and classify a mapped feature. Examples: direct on site observation, GPS on site, air photo mapping, satellite imagery.

Metadata scope terms

Controlled vocabulary Codes for metadata hierarchy level. Values: “attribute”, “collectionSession”, “dataset”, “feature”, “feildSession”, “model”, “nonGeographicDataset”, “series”, “service”, other values allowed.

Vocabulary relationship role terms

Term that specifies the relationship between source and target vocabulary terms (subset of role terms on GeologicRelationType). Terms should be constructed so the source to target reading of the relationship is clear, for example SourceIsBroaderTerm, SourceIsChildOfTarget.
