Granoblastic foliation   Foliation in a metamorphic rock with a granoblastic texture. Granoblastic (or crystalloblastic) means consisting of crystals entirely of metamorphic origin, of any size from very fine to very large, with grain bountaries that typically meet at 120° triple junctions. Fabric elements may be layering or aligned tabular crystals or crystal aggregates.  This concept is used to distinguish foliations that are the result of crystal plastic or brittle deformation (e.g. mylonitic foliations, in which recrystallization is slow relative to deformation rates) from foliation produced in environments in which recrystallization is the dominant factor determining grain shape. Weak protomylonite foliation   Protomylonite foliation defined by shape of deformed, originally equant grains, in which aspect ratio of deformed grains is generally less than 3 to 1 (longest to shortest axis). Incipient mylonitic foliation, in which the planar aspect of the foliation is  apparent on some sections normal to the foliation and may be quite subtle on other surfaces. Commonly grades into a linear fabric  (weak 'l' tectonite) SMR not standard   foliation
Weak schistosity   Weak shape fabric defined by aligned mineral grains whose crystal habit imparts the tabular shape defining the foliation, in cases where less than 50 percent of rock consists of mineral grains defining the foliation. Note that weak schistosity is also a kind of schistosity, and in many cases, a kind of crystalloblastic foliation; the representation of these structures in a single-parent hierarchy requires arbitrary decisions for presentation. SMR not standard   foliation
Weak shape foliation   Planar foliation defined by aligned mineral grains or grain aggregates that have a tabular, prismatic, or ellipsiodal geometry, in cases where less than 50 percent of rock consists of mineral grains defining the foliation. Shape of mineral grains due to crystal habit, deformation, some combination of these or not specified, and lacks denotation that tectonic matrix (see mylontic foliation defintion) is present. SMR not standard   foliation
