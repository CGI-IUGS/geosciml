The FabricDescription class (GeoSciML UML model, EarthMaterial diagram) associates geologic structures specified by the 'FabricType' term with a RockMaterial (ie, tectonic, metamorphic, sedimentary, igneous fabrics or textures).  FabricDescription is distinguished from Particle Geometry based on the criteria that Particle Geometry is preserved if a CompoundMaterial is disaggregated, while FabricDescription is not defined if the material is disaggregated. The v2 RC4 model (090108) does not allow a CompoundMaterial to have a FabricDescription.
Categories in this vocabulary are for use in specifying geologic structures that are present in gsml:RockMaterials  (e.g. rock description, sediment description). There are (at least) two use cases here-- describing lithologic components of a geologic unit, in which case the implication of the geologic structure association is that the structure is an ubiquitous characteristic property of the described material (fabric). The second case is for a RockMaterial instance associated with an individual sample, in which case the FabricDescription property is simply an assertion that in this particular sample, this structure is present.
There is a fuzzy boundary between observations described as a compound material and observations described as a geologic unit. The conceptual framework of GeoSciML is based on the idea that a Geologic Unit is composed of one or more RockMaterials. The operational decision that must be made in constructing a GeologicUnit description is what 'unit cell' to consider representative of the material that composes the geologic unit. The idea is that the chosen 'RockMaterial' unit should be representative of the geologic unit in any outcrop. 
Terms in this vocabulary used in GeoSciML documents should be the gsml:identifiers for concepts in a GeologicVocabulary, indirectly providing a definition of the term and a link to prototype GeologicStructure descriptions if they exist. Use gml:description on the FabricDescription to capture any free text to indicate any specific or peculiar features of the described fabric in the RockMaterial. Some of the structure types from this vocabulary may also be applied as a geologic structure type associated with a GeologicUnit through a GeologicRelation, but because of the different modeling of the relationship between GeologicStructure and GeologicUnit, that link would have to be to the GeologicStructure instances that are prototypes for ControlledConcepts in the GeologicVocabulary containing the terms used for CompoundMaterial.fabric.FabricDescription.FabricType.
GEOLOGIC STRUCTURE: (NADM C1, 2004)  "A configuration of matter in the Earth based on describable inhomogeneity, pattern, or fracture in an EarthMaterial   The identity of a GeologicStructure is independent of the material that is the substrate for the structure. There are almost always strong dependencies between the nature of the EarthMaterial substrate and the kinds of GeologicStructure that may be present. A disaggregated heap of particles does not have structure, and can only be described in terms of the mineralogy and geometrical character of the constituent particles.  GeologicStructures are more likely to be found in, and are more persistent in, consolidated materials than in unconsolidated materials. Properties like clast-supported, matrix-supported, and graded bed that do not involve orientation are considered kinds of GeologicStructure because they depend on the configuration of parts of a rock body."
FABRIC. (NADM C1, 2004) "Penetrative GeologicStructure defined by the relationship between constituent elements in an EarthMaterial.   Fabric denotes a pattern, defined by one or more EMConstituents or GSDefiningElements, that is present throughout a rock body when considered at some scale. Fabric is defined based on the average configuration of many constituents. Penetrative denotes that these constituents are distributed throughout the rock volume at the scale of observation [Passchier and Trouw, 1998], and are repeated at distances that are small relative to the scale of the whole, such that they can be considered to pervade the whole uniformly and be present at every point (Turner and Weiss [1963] p. 21-24; Hobbs and others [1976], p. 73; Jackson [1997]; Passchier and Trouw [1998]). Fabric is distinguished from ParticleGeometry based on the criteria that ParticleGeometry is preserved if a CompoundMaterial is disaggregated, while Fabric is not defined if the material is disaggregated. STRUCTURE, FABRIC, AND TEXTURE. The recently published IUGS metamorphic vocabulary has introduced a new variation in usage of the terms fabric and texture. The IUGS work was focused on metamorphic rocks, whereas this vocabulary is meant to be applicable to any RockMaterial that might be described in an interchange data set. IUGS usage--
Structure (IUGS, Fettes and Desmons, 2007) "arrangement of the parts of a rock mass irrespective of scale, including spatial relationships between the parts, their relative size and shape and the internal features of the parts."
Texture (IUGS, Fettes and Desmons, 2007) "(a) Term defining the relative size, shape and spatial interrelationship between grains and internal featuers of grains (synonomous with microstructure). (b) Term denoting the presence of perferred orinetation on the microscopic scale. Synomous with microfabric." Not recommended usage favor microstructure or microfabric to avoid ambiguity.
Fabric (IUGS, Fettes and Desmons, 2007, p151) " Relative orientation of parts of a rock mass...This is commonly used to refer to the crystallographic and/or shape orientation of mineral grains or groups of grains, but can also be used on a larger scale." 
Discussion: NADM C1 'GeologicStructure' is fundamentally defined based on 'describable inhomogeneity', while IUGS (Fettes and Desmons, 2007) 'Structure' is fundamentally defined based on 'arrangement'. In the broad context of geoscience, including structural geology as well as metamorphic rocks, the NADM C1 definition is favored for GeoSciML vocabulary usage because it more clearly includes faults and fractures. 'Arrangement' of parts is one approach to describing inhomogeneity.  The NADM C1 term fabric denotes penetrative NADM GeologicStructure, with no restriction that the arrangement is defined by particle orientation, as is included in the IUGS 'Fabric' concept. NADM C1 'relationship between constituent elements' is considered synomous with 'arrangement', thus the IUGS 'Structure' concept actually maps quite closely to the NADM C1 'Fabric' concept, and the two terms are considered synomous here.
